2 · Why the original framing is dead

From the v1 audit · Section 4 (Bridges)

"Bridges that were originally promised (HQ Contacts as SF Ops CRM seed) are dead." SF Ops shipped its own customer/contact model with aggregators (e.g. Property Management Inc), end-clients (the actual property), and polymorphic bill_to relationships. HQ's flat contact row can't represent any of that.

SF Ops customers serve a domain need (who do I invoice for this WO?) that doesn't translate to a generic HQ-level address book. Sonja: confirm this characterization before locking.

3 · The new question — is SF Solutions still in play?

SF Solutions exists at the platform entity level — the entity enum 'sf' in HQ tags projects/tasks/knowledge as belonging to it. But there's no product for SF Solutions yet (no app, no workflow, no DB schema). Sonja's hold on audit row 10 was: "Even though SF Ops diverged, HQ Contacts might seed a future SF Solutions CRM."

The question is whether that seed framing actually buys anything, or whether keeping the framing alive creates the wrong kind of pressure on the contacts feature now (build a real CRM!) and on a future SF Solutions product (be compatible with the seed!).

4 · Options matrix

10-C · Reframe as Personal CRM REC Position as Sonja's HQ-personal address book. "People I've shared docs with or added by hand." No platform-level seed promise. Schema unchanged. Keep the share-forwarding integration path open. Future SF Solutions builds its own customer model from scratch when needed. Cleaner separation of concerns. HQ Contacts stays useful for what it actually does. ~1 hour · nav label + page header copy + this memo
10-D · Keep narrow + decide later No code change. Document that no framing was locked in this round; revisit if Sonja actually starts a SF Solutions product. Same risk as today — bridge promise stays implicit, may zombie back into roadmap conversations. 0 · doc only
My take · why 10-C

10-A loses Sonja's existing 3 contacts and the share_forwarding_requests integration point (the forwarding flow assumes a contact registry to write to). 10-B locks future SF Solutions to a schema designed for a personal address book. 10-D defers the question, which is exactly how the original SF Ops seed framing rotted.

10-C is the smallest realignment that makes the feature honest about what it is today (Sonja's personal address book for share targets) without claiming territory it can't deliver on (be the CRM for an unbuilt product). Future SF Solutions remains free to build the right shape from scratch.

5 · What's NOT in this spec

  • The share-forwarding flow. Already exists per migration 20260425000004; not in scope for the framing decision.
  • Multi-org contact sharing. Org-scoped today; cross-org is a Sprint 12+ question if it ever comes up.
  • A real SF Solutions product. Out of scope. Sonja: if you start one, do a fresh CRM spec then.
  • Contact import/export tooling. If 10-A is picked, an export step is needed first (3 rows is trivial to copy by hand though).
